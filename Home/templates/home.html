CCAT Exam

C-DAC Common Admission Test (C-CAT) is the entrance test that is conducted in June (for August admissions) and December (for February admissions) to C-DAC's various 6 month long PG Diploma courses.
How to register for C-CAT
Admissions to all PG Diploma courses of C-DAC are done through C-DAC's computerized Common Admission Test (C-CAT). Every year, C-CAT is usually conducted in June (for August admissions) and December (for February admissions).
Eligibility Criteria for PG Diploma Courses of C-DAC
  • Graduate in Engineering or equivalent (BE/B.Tech/4-year B.Sc Engg/AMIE/DoEACC B Level) in IT/Computer Science/Electronics/Telecommunications/Electrical/Instrumentation.
    • OR
  • Post Graduate Degree in Engineering Sciences with a corresponding basic degree (M.Sc in Computer Science, IT, Electronics).

CDAC End Exam (CCEE)

After completion of all the module of PG-DAC, a written examination CCEE (Centralised Course End Examination) will be held, which will test the knowledge of the students about the theoretical concepts of each module and it is a compulsory part of the evaluation. Conducting CCEE involves performing duty with responsibility. A small mistake in the process may hamper the whole system.
Guidelines of CCEE
CCEE will be conducted normally before the commencement of Project work of PG-DAC.
The written exam will be of 60 minutes duration. It will consist of objective questions. A typical objective type exam paper will contain the following type of questions:-

  • Multiple choice
  • Yes or No
  • True or False
For the pure objective type question paper, there will be 50 objective type questions with 4 maximum answer options having only one correct option. The value of each objective type question is of one mark only. There will not be any negative marks for the wrong answers given by the students.
